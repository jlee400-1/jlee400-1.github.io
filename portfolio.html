GATHER TOWN - LOKAH LIVING MUSEUM

Course project in Changing Museum Practice of Living Culture at NTU

Field Research Content Design Exhibit Curation Digital Interpretion

This online exhibition was crafted to unveil the traditional hunting culture within Taiwanese indigenous communities. Our approach prioritized accuracy and cultural sensitivity, with comprehensive field research informing the content.

Utilizing Gather Town as our chosen platform, we developed various assets, interactions, and narratives. Our objective was to vividly portray the daily life of indigenous communities, offering visitors an immersive experience.

DIGITAL MUSEUM - THE CULTURE OF SAKIZAYA

Course project in Museum Learning at NTU

Digital Content Curation Social Media

The virtual exhibition spotlighted the cultural history of the Sakizaya, an indigenous community in Taiwan. Our exploration delved into the domain of content design, resulting in a concise overview encapsulating the attire, cuisine, and festivals of the Sakizaya community —a sort of Cliff's Notes version.

Using Instagram, we delivered concise introductions accompanied by graphic arts to reach a wide audience. By Leveraging the platform's story and highlight features, we effectively disseminated additional in-depth information, fostering a more profound understanding of the community's rich traditions and modern relevance.

THEMED BOOK EXHIBITION - THE MYSTERY OF TRADITIONAL PASTRY

Intern project in NTU LIS Library

Event Planning Information Design Exhibit Curation

The themed book exhibition centered around Taiwanese traditional pastries. To bring the books to life and immerse visitors in local history and culture, books highlighting distinctive pastries were collected and correlated with specific cities on a map of Taiwan. Complementing the exhibition’s narrative, a mascot was designed to enhance the overall experience.
