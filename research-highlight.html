Digital Curation

  • Rabbit's Adventure: Exploring the World of Dough Figurine

    • The exhibition aspires to connect visitors with the exhibition theme through the knowledge of traditional folk crafts and the emotions embedded within them. It aims to promote the art of dough figurine making and the experiences of local artisans to a wider audience. Through engaging with the exhibition content, interacting with fellow visitors, and reflecting on oneself, one can perceive the profound depth and vitality of the culture it represents.

    Digital Interpretation: Interactive Game

  • Design Principles and Methods
    • Conducted literature reviews and field research to create contents
    • Suitted the target audience, children, with simplified and child-friendly language
    • Used interactive elements like character “Rui Rui (the rabbit)” to engage young visitors and encourage interaction
    • Enhanced interactive experience through hands-on unit featuring dough-making instructions, accompanied by photos and videos
    • Future plans include developing separate guides for parents and children, and considering exhibition venues and timing for better audience engagement

  • Digital Curation Best Practice: A Case Study of “Exploring the World of Dough Figurine”
    • Author Lee, Jo-Ling; Chen, Shu-Jiun
    Publication Date 2024-12-08
    Publisher 3rd Conference of LIS and Practices (CoLISP 2023)
    Pages 171-180
    Abstract Through professional skill training and project-based practice in a digital curation course, this study successfully completed the exhibition "Rabbit's Adventure: Exploring the World of Dough Figurine" as a demonstration of best practices in digital curation. In addition to presenting the methods and tools required for the digitization of cultural heritage, the study also deconstructs the practical applications of digital exhibitions. On the other hand, this paper further explores and summarizes the competencies and core skills necessary for digital curation. The results indicate that curators equipped with foundational curatorial concepts and practices, organizational planning, and digital content development abilities are better able to construct effective digital exhibitions. These findings are corroborated by the exhibition experiences of visitors, including knowledge enhancement, impressive interactive design, and immersive experiences. This paper proposes that the above-mentioned indicators be incorporated into digital curation pedagogy as recommendations for curriculum content planning.
    Keywords Digital Curation, Cultural Heritage Digitization, Open Museum
    Citation Lee, J. L., Chen, S. J.(2023, Dec 8-9). Digital Curation Best Practice: A Case Study of “Exploring the World of Dough Figurine” [conference paper]. 3rd Conference of LIS and Practices, Taipei, Taiwan.
